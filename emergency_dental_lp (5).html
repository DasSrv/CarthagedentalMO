❓ FAQ

Your Questions Answered

What counts as a dental emergency?
Any dental problem causing severe pain, swelling, bleeding, or tooth loss is an emergency — including toothaches, broken teeth, lost crowns, abscesses, and knocked-out teeth. When in doubt, call us.
Can I be seen today without an appointment?
Yes! We accept walk-ins for emergency cases. Call ahead if you can so we can have a room ready, but if you're in severe pain, just come in.
Do you accept insurance for emergencies?
We accept most major plans including Delta Dental, Cigna, Aetna, MetLife, and many more. Our team verifies your coverage before treatment at no charge to you.
What if I can't afford treatment today?
We offer 0% financing through CareCredit and flexible payment plans. We never turn away a patient in pain because of finances — let's talk about options.
My tooth got knocked out — what do I do right now?
Hold the tooth by the crown, rinse with water, and try to reinsert it. If not possible, store it in milk or saliva — and call us immediately. Within 60 minutes gives the best chance of saving it.
Are emergency dental visits more expensive?
Emergency exams start with a standard exam fee covered by most insurance. We review all costs before any treatment. Zero surprise charges — ever.
